The World's first real-time tester

Reducing the impact of sewage contamination on regular water users

What is Aquarius?

The Aquarius™ water quality tester makes checking the health of your local bathing waters instant and effortless! The intuitive user interaction, easy to use testing cartridges and wearable strap make this the perfect addition to your watersports adventures!

The Problem

UK water users are just as likely to become ill from seawater as they were in the 1990’s!

  • Bathing water samples are only taken on 20 out of the 153 days in the bathing season; only 13% of the bathing season is tested.
  • The current testing regime does not account for changes in microbial concentrations, which happen over a matter of hours during sewage overflow events.
  • Current testing only looks for bacterial parameters, however, research shows that viruses are more common in bathing waters.

The Solution

Aquarius™: A simple and intuitive water quality tester that aims to reduce the impact of sewage contamination on regular water users. It addresses this impact by testing for Norovirus ad E. Coli in real-time, uploading the data to an app database to make it accessible to anyone at any time.

Simple & Portable

Easy to use and intended for anyone watersport enthusiast, it communicates test results through a single coloured LED, making it easy to understand whilst in the water. More detailed results are uploaded to the Aquarius™ app to be checked after your swim, surf or kayak. Aquarius™ can be worn as a wearable whilst in the water or attached to your equipment, so there is no worry about where to keep it whilst in the water or having to leave things on the beach.

Microfluidics Technology

Aquarius™ tests for both Norovirus and E. Coli, two key indicators for sewage contamination witin bathing waters. It does this using two reusable microfluidic cartridges that house relevant antigens, any positive reaction between the bathing water sample and the antigens is detected by multiple LEDs to determine the level of pathogens present in the sample. This quantity is then translated into one of the four UK government classifications:

  • Excellent: No pathogen detected (≤250cfu/100ml - 95th percentile)
  • Good: Minimal level of pathogen detected (≤500cfu/100ml - 95th percentile)
  • Sufficient: Medium level of pathogen detected (≤500cfu/100ml - 90th percentile)
  • Poor: High level of pathogen detected (>500cfu/100ml)

Usability

Aquarius™ is wirelessly charged through a hub that runs off mains power, this hub can be placed anywhere in the home on a flat surface or a wall. In the lower section of the hub, the testing cartridges can be inserts for ultraviolet cleaning between uses.
